Island House - bringing the outside in

Newly refurbished, Island House offers vistas aplenty to make the most of. Whether you’ve been out rambling all day, quietly scoping the shoreline for waders or exploring the island’s religious heritage, those views don’t stop as you retreat behind the double-glazing, to the comfort of a sofa and a roaring open fire.

Gaze out upon the raw beauty of the Northumbrian coastline and its varied bird life, as well as at the lapping Lindisfarne shoreline, across Pilgrim’s Way and out to sea.

Let us show you around…

Lounge

Flooded with light, French windows offer superb sea views from the comfort of the sofa. Bask in front of the open fire with a book, or enjoy all of the necessary mod cons – a wifi router, Bluetooth stereo system, TV and DVD player. Sliding doors offer access to a sunny private terrace and garden perfect for watching the sunset.
An open tread staircase leads upstairs from the lounge, with views of the Priory and St Mary’s Church from the landing.

Study

A book, a cup of tea, the North Sea. Off the lounge, find our cosy library, filled with hundreds of books. Read a book and watch the clouds pass by, or train your telescope onto the seals on the sandbanks and sanderlings skittering in and out of the wake, through the floor-to-ceiling window.

Dining kitchen

Watch the tides change and see those waders come flocking in, with uninterrupted views across the sea and causeway.
The well-equipped, modern kitchen diner offers a dishwasher and double oven, for easy catering. Sliding glass doors open onto the patio and garden, perfect for a wander with a mug of tea, listening to the waves lap the shore.

Double Bedroom

This cosy downstairs bedroom features a comfortable pocket-sprung 4’6” double bed, and offers storage space and a hairdryer. Pure cotton sheets, down duvets and down (or hypoallergenic) pillows offer sumptuous comfort (in every bedroom).

King size en-suite bedroom

This first upstairs bedroom offers panoramic sea views over to the mainland, the causeway, Cuthbert’s Island and across the sand dunes to the Cheviot Hills. The pocket-sprung king-size bed offers a luxurious night’s sleep.
Enjoy a relaxing bath in the smart en-suite, with two raised windows offering views across the shore. The room offers ample storage and a hairdryer.

Twin bedroom

Two confortable 3’6” single beds offer flexible sleeping arrangements, suitable for adults and children alike. Like the other bedrooms, the twin-bed offers storage and a hairdryer. This bedroom also enjoys fantastic vistas over the sea.

House bathroom

A luxurious fully tiled walk in wet room with underfloor heating and towel warmer.

Garden

The lawn-covered rear garden is west-southwest facing, and completely private. It offers fabulous views and is sunny from mid-morning until spectacular sunset.
Help yourself to fresh herbs snipped from the herb beds. A private, secure yard to the side of the house features the coal bunker and a safe storage space for bikes.

Utility room

A washer and tumble dryer, extra fridge and freezer and ironing board provide the opportunity to do any laundry and store any extra food supplies.

General

Park up to three cars in Island House’s private drive.
From the front door, enter the lobby and find a useful coat cupboard with boot racks to dry out any wet gear, and a downstairs WC.
The house is fully double-glazed throughout, and features oil-fired central heating as well as an open fire.
