Lindisfarne’s legacy

Lying just off the Northeast coast of England, Holy Island pulls away from Northumberland and up towards Berwick-upon-Tweed, just over the Scottish border.

This unspoilt island is imbued with history. Built by Irish monk Saint Aidan as early as 634, the priory then became the seat of ancient Christian evangelism. In the later 8th century, the internationally acclaimed Lindisfarne Gospels were created here, a spectacular set of illuminated religious writings.

Saint Cuthbert, patron saint of Northumbria (and protector of birds), was also bishop of Lindisfarne. The eider duck, regularly spotted from our windows, bobbing at the shoreline, is nicknamed ‘Cuddy Duck’ after St Cuthbert. The island’s appeal, both theologically and ornithologically, is unsurpassed! But theology and ornithology are only a part of the fantastic past, and present, of Holy Island.
