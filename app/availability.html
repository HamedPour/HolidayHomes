Holy Island and Beyond

This Lindisfarne holiday cottage has something to offer, summer or winter. Fabulous gardens make the most of bright summer’s days, but the same inspiring views are accessible in less wonderful conditions too, with floor to ceiling (double-glazed) windows in many rooms.

There’s so much to see and do, both on the island and on the mainland.

When you choose to leave Island House’s comforts, wander the island and its shops, dip a toe into the waters of the North Sea, or wander the Priory’s ancient ruins. You can even walk to the mainland at low tide (perfectly safe, as long as you stick to the path and remain aware of the tides).

Across the causeway, your options are endless. Immerse yourself in history by exploring the wonderful castles, from jaw-dropping Bamburgh, to Dunstanburgh’s iconic ruins, to Alnwick castle, set of Hogwart’s School in the Harry Potter films! Alternatively, get your walking boots on and head for the Cheviots for stunning, hilly views, or the beach for miles upon miles of well-kept shoreline. Berwick and Alnwick offer an afternoon of retail therapy, and even the bright lights of Newcastle or Edinburgh are both less than 1h30 away. Seahouses offers boat trips to see the puffins and seals at the Farne Islands, which is most spectacular when the terns and eider ducks come in to nest in late spring.

Northumberland also offers real Geordie hospitality. Enjoy a beer at local pubs and breweries such as Low Newton’s The Ship Inn, eat fabulous pub grub at Craster’s The Jolly Fisherman or enjoy a real treat at Bamburgh’s The Lobster Pot.

If the weather outside is too forbidding, take refuge in Alnwick’s Barter Books, a converted railway station-now-bookshop, with its open fires, ‘station café’ and reams of books, old and new. Alternatively, head over to the fascinating (and very eccentric!) Chillingham Castle, or visit Cragside, near Rothbury, to explore the extraordinary house and gardens of famous industrialist William Armstrong.

Extras

We are here to make your stay as enjoyable as it can possibly be! To that end, we can offer added extra services and activities – simply get in touch when you make your booking.

activities

The region offers a plethora of activities, from bird watching trips to island tours to Northern Lights excursions to photography courses. Get in touch if you have something in mind and we would be happy to help organise it.

local produce

The island (and the North East coast generally) offers exquisitely fresh seafood, with lobster and crab being local specialities. There are also several fine coffee merchants and bakeries on the island. We can get any of these local products in, ready for your stay – let us know what you’d like and we would be more than happy to help.
