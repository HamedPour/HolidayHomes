Holy Island and Beyond

This Lindisfarne holiday cottage has something to offer, summer or winter. Fabulous gardens make the most of bright summer’s days, but the same inspiring views are accessible in less wonderful conditions too, with floor to ceiling (double-glazed) windows in many rooms.

There’s so much to see and do, both on the island and on the mainland.

When you choose to leave Island House’s comforts, wander the island and its shops, dip a toe into the waters of the North Sea, or wander the Priory’s ancient ruins. Visit Lindisfarne Castle and Gertrude Jeykll’s garden, view the whole island from The Lookout on the Heugh. You can even walk to the mainland across the Pilgrims path at low tide (perfectly safe, as long as you stick to the path and remain aware of the tides).

Across the causeway, your options are endless. Immerse yourself in history by exploring the wonderful castles, from jaw-dropping Bamburgh, to Dunstanburgh’s iconic ruins, to Alnwick castle, set of Hogwart’s School in the Harry Potter films! Alternatively, get your walking boots on and head for the Cheviots for stunning, hilly views, or the beaches for miles upon miles of well-kept shoreline. Berwick and Alnwick offer an afternoon of retail therapy, and the bright lights of Newcastle or Edinburgh are both less than 1h30 away. Seahouses offers boat trips to see the puffins and seals at the Farne Islands, which is most spectacular when the terns and eider ducks come in to nest in late spring.

Northumberland also offers real Geordie hospitality. Enjoy a beer at local pubs and breweries such as Low Newton’s The Ship Inn, eat fabulous pub grub at Craster’s The Jolly Fisherman or enjoy a real treat at Bamburgh’s The Lobster Pot.

If the weather outside is too forbidding, take refuge in Alnwick’s Barter Books, a converted railway station-now-bookshop, with its open fires, ‘station café’ and reams of books, old and new. Alternatively, head over to the fascinating (and very eccentric!) Chillingham Castle, or visit Cragside, near Rothbury, to explore the extraordinary house and gardens of famous industrialist William Armstrong.
